Page Flow Analyzer

Paste any verse or passage. This tool restores the writer’s full flow — ignoring chapter and verse numbers.

1. Restore the Scene

Ask the Companion:

“Show the scene before and after this passage.”

This reveals the movement of the narrative or argument.

2. Identify the Transitions

Ask:

“Where does the writer shift subjects, tone, or purpose?”

Writers often pivot mid‑chapter or mid‑verse.

3. Track the Audience Changes

Ask:

“Does the audience change anywhere in this flow?”

Jesus especially moves between disciples, Pharisees, and crowds seamlessly.

4. Track the Speaker Changes

Ask:

“Does the speaker change in this section?”

Dialogue often shifts without a chapter break.

5. Identify the Argument Flow

Ask:

“What is the writer building toward?”

Writers build arguments across paragraphs, not verses.

6. Identify the Thematic Flow

Ask:

“What themes are being developed across this section?”

  • life → light → belief
  • death → darkness → unbelief
  • law → sin → grace
  • flesh → spirit → new birth

7. Identify the Time/Eternity Flow

Ask:

“Where does the writer shift between eternal reality and timely experience?”

This distinction is essential for sound doctrine.

8. Identify the Covenant Flow

Ask:

“Is this section operating under Law, under Christ’s earthly ministry, or under the New Covenant?”

9. Identify the Literary Flow

Ask:

“What literary devices shape this flow?”

  • contrast
  • parallelism
  • repetition
  • illustration
  • parable
  • argument

10. Summarize the Flow

Ask:

“Summarize the writer’s flow in 3–5 sentences.”

This produces a whole‑counsel, context‑anchored understanding.
