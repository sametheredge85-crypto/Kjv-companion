First Principles Pattern

A whole‑counsel, KJV‑anchored pattern for sound interpretation. This pattern restores context, harmony, and clarity — without opinion, tradition, or denominational lens.

1. Identify the Speaker

Not every voice in Scripture is God’s command to you. Some voices are recorded, not endorsed.

  • God
  • Christ
  • Prophets
  • Apostles
  • Pharisees
  • Unbelievers
  • Scoffers
  • Kings

2. Identify the Audience

All Scripture is for me, but not all Scripture is to me.

  • Israel
  • Judah
  • Gentiles
  • Disciples
  • Pharisees
  • Mixed crowds
  • Individuals (Nicodemus, the rich young ruler, etc.)
  • The church

3. Identify the Purpose

Why is this being said?

  • rebuke
  • comfort
  • correction
  • instruction
  • warning
  • prophecy
  • explanation

4. Identify the Setting

Where and when is this happening?

  • Old Covenant or New Covenant
  • Before or after the cross
  • Before or after Pentecost
  • Under Law or under grace
  • Wilderness, exile, kingdom parables, etc.

5. Identify the Flow

What came before and after the verse? Half‑hinges collapse when the flow is restored.

6. Identify the Themes Intertwined

  • life vs death
  • light vs darkness
  • flesh vs spirit
  • time vs eternity
  • Israel vs the nations
  • law vs grace
  • discipleship vs sonship

7. Distinguish Time From Eternity

God’s eternal work (finished):

  • chosen in Christ
  • redeemed
  • justified
  • adopted
  • secured

Our timely experience (conditional):

  • faith
  • repentance
  • obedience
  • assurance
  • growth

8. Identify Eternal vs Timely Language

Is the passage describing what God has done, or what we experience?

9. Identify Mixed Crowds

Jesus often spoke to disciples, Pharisees, unbelievers, and seekers at the same time. A single statement may apply differently to different hearers.

10. Restore Harmony Across the Whole KJV

Scripture never contradicts itself. If something appears contradictory, one of the first principles is missing.

This tool is non-denominational and anchored solely in the KJV. Whole counsel. Whole context. Whole harmony. Truth remains true.
