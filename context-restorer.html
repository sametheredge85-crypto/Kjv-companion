Context Restorer

Paste any verse or passage. This tool restores the full context — who is speaking, to whom, about what, and why.

How to Restore Context

After pasting your verse, copy it and ask the Companion:

“Restore the full context of this passage from the writer’s perspective.”

The Companion will identify:

  • Speaker — who is talking?
  • Audience — to whom are they speaking?
  • Purpose — why is this being said?
  • Setting — where and when is this happening?
  • Flow — what came before and after?
  • Mixed crowds — believers? unbelievers? disciples? Pharisees?
  • Individuals vs groups — Nicodemus? Israel? the apostles?
  • Time vs eternity — what God has done vs what we experience now

Why This Matters

All Scripture is for us, but not all Scripture is to us. Most errors — including universalism — depend on ignoring:

  • the speaker
  • the audience
  • the purpose
  • the setting
  • the flow of the passage

When the whole context is restored, half‑hinges collapse and the harmony becomes clear.

This tool does not promote a system. It simply reveals the consistency already present in the KJV.
